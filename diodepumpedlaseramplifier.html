Summary

Achieving multi-petawatt laser power requires substantial amplification. This project focuses on one of the earlier amplifier stages, which uses Nd:Glass as the gain medium pumped by laser diodes. We're using a slab amplifier geometry to increase the pumped area while maintaining sufficient beam aperture to avoid intensities sufficient to damage the gain medium. During this stage, the laser beam will be shaped into an oval profile to enable a multipass scheme. The final laser will incorporate approximately a dozen of these amplifiers. This project focuses on developing the prototype amplifier and finalizing the design before production of the final units. Our goals are to resolve all mechanical issues, conduct thermal testing to verify cooling system reliability, and measure gain with the Nd:Glass slab to confirm it meets optical requirements.

Project Goals

  • Iterate frame design to ensure it meets mechanical requirements
  • Conduct thermal tests to ensure watercooling system operation
  • Conduct optical tests to ensure the amplifier meets the gain requirements
  • Assemble supporting optical components to enable testing
